## STRUCTURE BLUEPRINT

### SECTION 1: OPENING — Context before claim
**Purpose:** Hook the reader with an observation, scene, or question. Establish why this matters.  
**Length:** 80–120 words (2 paragraphs)  
**Tone:** Evocative, unhurried. No "In this article, we will..." openers.  
**Keyword:** Primary keyword must appear in first 100 words.

> *[Write an opening that places the reader in a scene or position of curiosity. Avoid stating what the article is about. Instead, let them feel why it matters.]*

---

### SECTION 2: THE CORE INSIGHT — The thesis
**Purpose:** State Sovereign's considered perspective on the topic.  
**Length:** 100–150 words (2–3 paragraphs)  
**Tone:** Authoritative, specific. One strong claim supported immediately.

> *[Present Sovereign's distinctive point of view. Not "here are some tips" but "here is what we believe, and why."]*

---

### SECTION 3: DEPTH — Evidence and elaboration
**Purpose:** Support the thesis with concrete examples, case references, material specifics.  
**Length:** 250–400 words (3–5 paragraphs or structured subsections with H3s)  
**Tone:** Precise, generous with knowledge. Share the expertise.  
**Structure options:**
- H3 subheadings for multi-part exploration
- A short example project reference (anonymised or named with permission)
- Material, process, or principle breakdown

> *[This is where the post earns its length. Specificity is the differentiator — competitor content is generic here.]*

---

### SECTION 4: THE GALLERIA LENS — Application to our approach
**Purpose:** Connect the topic back to how Sovereign thinks and works.  
**Length:** 100–150 words (2 paragraphs)  
**Tone:** Personal (studio voice), not self-promotional.

> *[How does this topic live in the studio's actual practice? A brief, honest perspective. Not a sales pitch — a perspective.]*

---

### SECTION 5: CLOSING + CTA — Invitation, not instruction
**Purpose:** Leave the reader with a resonant final thought. CTA is contextual.  
**Length:** 60–100 words (1–2 paragraphs + CTA)  
**Tone:** Warm, unhurried. The CTA invites; never demands.

> *[Close with a thought that reframes or extends the opening. The CTA should feel like a natural next step, not a commercial break.]*

**CTA options (choose one):**
- "Explore our completed commissions to see this approach in practice. →"
- "If this resonates with how you're thinking about your space, we'd welcome a conversation."
- "See how this principle shaped [Project Name] →" *(if relevant project exists)*
