# 🌍 Bilingual Ad Localization (MENA Mastery)

## Purpose
Enforce professional standards for adapting global advertising for the **MENA market**. This skill focuses on the **Cultural Intelligence** required to translate "Intent" and "Emotion" rather than just words, specializing in **Bilingual AR/EN synchronization**.

---

## Technique 1 — Cultural Sensitivity & "Safe-Ad" Logic

### The MENA Cultural Filter
- **Visuals**: Respect regional dress codes and social norms (e.g., family-centric visuals perform 40% better). 
- **Religion & Holidays**: Align campaigns with **Ramadan**, **EID**, and **National Days**; these periods account for up to 60% of annual consumer spend.
- **Color Psychology**: Green is associated with "Growth/Prosperity" whereas Yellow can signify "Luxury" or "Desert Wealth" in specific contexts.

---

## Technique 2 — Dialect-Specific Conversion (The Masri/Khaliji Delta)

- **Khaliji (GCC)**: Use for Luxury, Real Estate, and Formal Business. Tone should be "Respectful-Elite."
- **Masri (EG)**: Use for FMCG, Entertainment, and General Consumer goods. Tone should be "Friendly-Humorous" (Leveraging `skills:16-content-dominance/regional-cultural/skills/egyptian-masri-cultural-hooks`).
- **MSA (Modern Standard Arabic)**: Use ONLY for Legal, Medical, or News-based advertising; it is often too "Cold" for social media conversions.

---

## Technique 3 — Bilingual Layout Synchronization

- **RT-to-LT Physics**: Ensure the "Visual Flow" in the Arabic ad mirrors the "Decision Path" of the English version.
- **Copy Length**: Arabic text is typically 20-30% longer than English. Design ads with "Dynamic Text Containers" to avoid overflow or crowding.

## 🛡️ Critical Failure Modes (Anti-Patterns)

| Anti-Pattern | Result | Correction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Literal Translation** | Loss of "Flavor" | Use "Transcreation" — rewrite the hook to fit the local idiom. |
| **Ignoring Local Trends** | "Out-of-touch" brand | Monitor local hashtags and social trends to ensure the ad feels "Current." |
| **English-First Thinking** | RTL layout breaks | Design the Arabic version of the ad *alongside* the English one, not as a secondary afterthought. |
