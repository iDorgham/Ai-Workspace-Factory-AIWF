# 📜 Egypt Contract Drafting Physics

## Purpose
Enforce professional legal standards for commercial drafting within the Arab Republic of Egypt. This skill governs the application of the **Egyptian Civil Code**, the logistics of **Notarization (Shahr El Aqari)**, and strict Arabic localization requirements.

---

## Technique 1 — The Egyptian Civil Code Foundation

### Good Faith & "Pacta Sunt Servanda"
- **Logic**: Under Egyptian Civil Law, the contract is "the law of the parties," provided it does not violate public policy or mandatory laws.
- **Drafting Implication**: Obligations must be explicitly stated. Implied terms are less favorably viewed by Egyptian courts compared to Common Law jurisdictions.

### Force Majeure vs. Hardship
- **Force Majeure**: Makes performance *impossible*; leads to termination.
- **Hardship (El-Zorouf El-Tari'a)**: Makes performance *exorbitantly burdensome*. Egyptian judges have statutory power to adjust contract terms if unforeseen events cause severe economic imbalance. Draft hardship clauses carefully.

---

## Technique 2 — Execution & Notarization (Shahr El Aqari)

- **Authentication Logic**: A contract signed privately (Urfi) is valid between parties but lacks "Date Certainty" against third parties. 
- **Protocol**: Critical contracts (Real Estate Leases, Share Pledges, Power of Attorneys) *must* be notarized at the Real Estate Registration and Authentication Office (Shahr El Aqari) to be fully enforceable against the state and third parties.
- **Bilingual Execution Constraint**: Shahr El Aqari will *only* notarize Arabic text. If a bilingual contract is submitted, only the Arabic section holds evidentiary value at the notary.

---

## Technique 3 — Corporate & Commercial Agents

- **Commercial Agency Law (No. 120 of 1982)**: A foreign company cannot directly distribute goods; they must appoint a 100% Egyptian Commercial Agent.
- **Exclusivity**: Be extremely precise with exclusivity clauses. Terminating a registered Egyptian commercial agent without "just cause" results in massive statutory compensation payouts, regardless of what the contract says.

---

## 🛡️ Critical Failure Modes (Anti-Patterns)

| Anti-Pattern | Result | Correction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **English-Only Drafting** | Court Rejection | Egyptian courts require all submissions in Arabic. An English-only contract must be officially translated by a sworn translator before a judge will read it. |
| **Foreign Arbitration Defaults** | Execution friction | While international arbitration (e.g., ICC, LCIA) is valid, executing a foreign arbitral award in Egypt can take years. For domestic deals, use CRCICA (Cairo Regional Centre for International Commercial Arbitration) for faster local enforcement. |
| **Loose Signature Blocks** | Piercing the corporate veil | Specify the signatory's capacity (e.g., "In his capacity as the Chairman holding the requisite signing powers as per the Commercial Register dated [X]"). |

## Success Criteria (Egypt Drafting QA)
- [ ] Governing law explicitly cites the "Laws of the Arab Republic of Egypt."
- [ ] "Date Certainty" (Notarization requirement) is assessed for the contract type.
- [ ] Arbitration clauses default to CRCICA for local/regional deals.
- [ ] Arabic terminology uses formal standard legal Arabic (Fusha), avoiding colloquialisms.
