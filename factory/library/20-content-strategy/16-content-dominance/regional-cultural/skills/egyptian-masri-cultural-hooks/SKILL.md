# 🇪🇬 Egyptian Masri Cultural Hooks (The Viral Engine)

## Purpose
Enforce professional and culturally resonant standards for Egyptian content creation. This skill specializes in the **"Masri" (Cairene)** dialect, focusing on the unique blend of **Social Humor, Sarcasm**, and **Professional Friendliness** required to dominate the Egyptian social and business landscape.

---

## Technique 1 — The Sarcasm Loop (Al-Kash)

### Humor-Driven Virality
- **Rule**: Never preach directly; use "Sarcastic Observation" to highlight a pain point.
- **Protocol**: Start with a common Egyptian "Problem" (e.g., bureaucracy, traffic, weather) and flip it into a self-deprecating joke before introducing the solution.
- **Key Phrasing**: Use idioms like *"Ya Ragel"* (Oh man), *"Eh el Kalam el Kibir da"* (What's this big talk), and *"Khalik fi el Mofid"* (Stick to the useful part).

---

## Technique 2 — Professional-Friendly Dialect

### Scaling the Tone
- **Social**: Ultra-casual, using trending "TikTok-Masri" slang and memes.
- **Business/Professional**: "White Masri" (Clean dialect). Use professional titles like *"Ya Basha"* or *"Ya Doctor"* with a friendly, inclusive undertone. Avoid overly formal Fusha (MSA) unless in a legal or religious context.

---

## 🛡️ Critical Failure Modes (Anti-Patterns)

| Anti-Pattern | Result | Correction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bad Slang Use** | "Cringe" / Outsider feel | Always verify the *recency* of a slang term; what was viral in Jan is "Dead" in March. |
| **Translation Fail** | Loss of wit | Do NOT translate English idioms (e.g., "Piece of cake") into Arabic; use the Masri equivalent *"As-hal min el Maya"*. |
| **Over-Fusha** | Alienating / Cold | Even in corporate blogs, inject "Masri-Soul" into the conversational bits to build trust. |
